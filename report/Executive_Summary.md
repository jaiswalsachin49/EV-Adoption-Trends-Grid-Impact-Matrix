# Executive Summary: Electric Vehicle Population Analysis

**Prepared For:** Infrastructure Planning Division & Utility Grid Operators  
**Region Focus:** Washington State

## 1. Context & Objective
The continuous mandate for clean alternative fuel vehicles introduces localized stress points to the municipal power grid. This analysis utilizes DMV registration data to visualize adoption trends across Washington State. Our goal is to extract actionable intelligence to fortify grid reliability and optimize the deployment of future charging station networks.

## 2. Key Findings (Derived from EDA)

- **Exponential Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth:** Data illustrates strong double-digit growth in annual EV registrations over the past 5 years. Sustained momentum requires immediate proactive cap-ex scaling. 
- **BEV vs PHEV Demand Distribution:** Battery Electric Vehicles (BEVs), which draw entirely from the grid without a secondary combustion engine, represent a significant portion of active registrations. This necessitates higher electrical service capacities at residential clusters.
- **Brand Dominance & Range Improvements:** Tesla historically dominates the region's overall 'Clean' category. A notable trend observed is that overall minimum battery range continues to increase, indicating an increase in long-haul capacity, which fundamentally shifts daytime vs nighttime charging behaviors.

## 3. Strategic Recommendations

1. **Targeted Substation Upgrades:** Zip codes with the highest concentration of EV registrations (identified through geospatial clustering) will require priority transformer upgrades.
2. **Incentivize Off-peak Charging:** Given the high concentration of fully electric fleets (BEVs), introducing aggressive time-of-use (TOU) rates between 10 PM and 6 AM is highly recommended to alleviate PM peak loading.
3. **Continuous Tableau Monitoring:** The integration of our cleaned database into our Tableau Server environment will allow grid operators to access live views of emerging zip-code level clusters month over month.
